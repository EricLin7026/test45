Project Idea:

For my project idea, I originally thought of creating a purely comedic self-help blog that parodied the often misinformed existing self-help blogs that advocate for things like salt crystals, but I realised that while it would be funny, it would not have that great of practical and real-world impact.

So instead I decided to go with creating an online plant simulation website, focused on exotic and often unreported plant life. Such a simulation would allow the user to choose the plant, customize environmental conditions such as rainfall, temperature, soil pH and nutritional value. I believe such a website would hold great educational value, especially for those in the younger generation (Primary-Secondary Schooling), as a beginner path to become interested in our ecosystem. Having an open-ended simulation allowing a user to customise and test all conditions they could think of could lead to greater interest in the biological field.

The skills required would range across both the IT and Biological fields, the site will actually need to be programmed, and someone needs to provide accurate information of plant growth to form the basis of the simulation. A proper hosting server would obviously be needed, as is an unused address. Not only that, in order for the program to purposed for a younger demographic, it needs to be artistically child-friendly and memorable, without compromising educational value, so a graphic designer is also needed.

The difficulties of this would be obtaining accurate statistical information about various types of plants to plug into the simulation, along with programming the simulation itself. The simulation would need to accurately display the likely real-world outcome of the plants it is simulating. Depending on the budget, however, the simulation will likely have to remain 2D, which does make HTML and CSS an ideal, if basic, method to use.

The benefits of such a program could be increased interest in plant life and an easier way to understand it, not through a stiff learning environment or pages upon pages of text, but through childhood curiosity to test and try every variable until they grow something that works. As a young child, I could remember logging onto sites like Cool Math’s Games during class and sneaking a few Flash games in. Whilst I don’t believe my project idea would ever be used by a child for recreation, it would be a useful tool for the suffering secondary school teacher who has nothing to give to their class and just tells them to play around with educational games under the pretence of: “Well, they’re learning aren’t they?”

If the site is successful, a future project could be expanding and creating a hosting site for various education-related games and simulations. Perhaps add a competitive factor to it, I remember I tried my hardest in math when the class was playing Mathletics. Overall, I hope such a project, if ever coming to fruition, would be a popular tool amongst students and teachers to both learn and teach the basics of plant systems.
